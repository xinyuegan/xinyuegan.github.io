Placing a Dot

Experimental Animation series / 2025 / 6 min 45 sec / Color / Sound

Statement

A screen is a passage connecting two dimensions and three.

One day, a dot suddenly appears on the screen. When the perspective shifts, we realize the dot is actually the side of a sphere. Another shift of perspective reveals that beneath the sphere is a square, and beside the square extend four lines.

It turns out to be a flat paper figure with a round head. But this is not the end. Inside the head of this round-headed figure, many more tiny figures emerge. They dance together, build houses with their bodies, and eventually tear open the rectangular screen itself—revealing countless other rectangular screens behind it, each filled with innumerable little round-headed figures.

And all of this begins with a single dot on the screen.
