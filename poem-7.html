Let Us Hide Before We're Born

You in your shadow—
shadow your amniotic fluid.
You touch past, present, every future
like ink staining itself dark.

Shadow walks through white cloth, each daylit hour,
yet dissolves in an unnamed rain-night.

That night:
a fetus
that died before birth.

You beneath the world—
in the gauzy chaos-light.
How beautiful.
