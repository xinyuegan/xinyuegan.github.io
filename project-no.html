No one is allowed in

Performance / 2023, 2025 / 1h

Statement

This was a semi-enclosed abandoned garage.

We first discovered it at night: the lights were on, and someone told us that we were not allowed to enter. During the day, the guard would leave, yet the lights remained on.

I found this strangely intriguing. We could not open the abandoned garage, and there was nothing inside that could be taken away. I began to imagine that everyone lives within their own time and space, guarding something invisible to others. Gradually they forget what it is they are guarding, until only the act of guarding itself remains.

In this performance, we walk along our own routes, repeating the same paths again and again. When two performers encounter each other, we say: “No one is allowed in.”

Then we continue walking.
