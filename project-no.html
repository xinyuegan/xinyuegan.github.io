No one is allowed in

Performance / 2023, 2025 / 1h

Statement

This is a collaborative performance art piece. We selected a semi-enclosed underground space—an intriguing site we were forbidden to enter. Thus, we centered the theme around “No Admittance,” aiming to express the very state of exclusion embodied by the space. Inside, we performed a set of actions: whenever two individuals encountered each other, they were required to utter the phrase “No Admittance,” thereby framing the other’s approach as a potential intrusion.

This act served both as a defense of personal space and an emphasis on the invisible barriers within human interaction. Each declaration of “No Admittance” became a performer’s assertion. The space itself revealed a kind of boundary against the outside world, and the performers’ actions sought to make this barrier visible. The experience invited viewers to re‑examine their own relationships with others, as well as the relationship between space and the body. Within the exhibition area, three screens displayed images and videos under the same “No Admittance” theme. Captured at different times in the same location, they suggested that the space perpetually maintained its “No Admittance” state.

We later reperformed this piece in an art museum, shifting the theme to “Have you found it?” The same participants walked along fixed paths, and upon meeting, would ask one another, “Have you found it?”—as if we were perpetually searching for something that does not exist.
