The Black Tree Bridge

Stage Installation / 2023 / Mixed media

Statement

Black Tree Bridge is a drama about mixed-race identity: the protagonist searches for belonging between China and the West, only to encounter the contradictions between self-constructed myths and reality. The question arises: is identity found at one end, or formed between both?

I designed a stage installation of the same name, with a bridge as the central visual and narrative motif. The bridge connects two shores but belongs to neither—mirroring the condition of mixed identity. The installation unfolds in three stages:

1. The bridge is unformed; cultural symbols intertwine, above and below water worlds overlap, reflecting confusion and rupture.

2. The bridge clarifies; history and culture seem to offer roots, yet stability is an illusion.

3. The bridge’s reflection forms a circle—no longer pointing to either side, but suggesting wholeness that transcends binary.

The circle embodies the work’s essence: identity is not a choice of one or the other; it is formed in between, continuously evolving. Mixed heritage is not “half and half,” but a simultaneous, generative multiplicity.
