Does Buddha Pray?

Experimental Animation / 2025 / 6 min 17 sec / Color / Sound

Statement

This animation was inspired by a journey to Shanxi, where I witnessed people throwing coins at the feet of a giant Buddha statue while murmuring: “Please bless me so that my wish may come true.”At that moment I felt strangely indignant on behalf of the Buddha. It seemed to have become a tool for exchanging money for wishes. If the statue had life, would it quietly pray: Please, let people stop asking me to grant their wishes.

Behind the great Buddha sit countless smaller statues—figures of all beings, symbolizing that everyone can become a Buddha, that Buddha-nature resides within every person. If people realized that the object of their prayers is, in essence, themselves—would they still pray? Do Buddhas pray?

So I imagined a scene: a group of small skeletons with Buddha heads praying to a giant skeleton with a Buddha head. Sometimes the giant skeleton becomes very small—small enough to be held in the hands of the little skeletons. Sometimes it becomes enormous—so large that they can only gaze up at it.

One day the little skeletons dance together, unaware that beneath their feet lies the head of the great Buddha skeleton.

This experimental animation received the 2025 European Design Awards and was exhibited at the TAM Figovec Outdoor Gallery in Ljubljana, Slovenia.
