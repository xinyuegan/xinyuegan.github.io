All Spirits' Portrait

The moment you raise your head,
 you hold a bear's might,
A lion's back becomes your own back's line,
A cat's eyes become the eyes you see the light,
Or like a house, its ridge upcurled and fine,
A steel bar, with its small, unnoticed dent.

Within your long-drawn shadow,
deep and vast,
The overture to all the world's forgetting lies.
Perhaps within that single lock of hair upcast,
There stirs a cat-tail's lively leap and grace,
Or a grass-blade's joy and ache that slowly rise
And
stir.
