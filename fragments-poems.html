Poems

All Spirits' Portrait

Within your long-drawn shadow, deep and vast, / The overture to all the world's forgetting lies.

Genesis

This world / Was born when the first eye opened, / Has passed when the last eye closed.

Compassion

Buddha is a giant, tender tongue, / On which all beings dwell and throng.

Between Sky and Earth

When the sky looms too high, set it down a notch.

Structure

Let us raise a vast house— / each soul its timber, nail, and tile.

Identity

The mound grew Grandfather's eyes, / Let Grandfather's sigh escape.

Let Us Hide Before We're Born

You in your shadow—shadow your amniotic fluid.
